About Smart Soil

Learn about our soil classification technology and how it can help improve agricultural outcomes.

Our Technology

Smart Soil uses a sophisticated Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) trained on thousands of soil images to accurately classify soil types. Our deep learning model can identify different soil types with high precision to help farmers and gardeners make informed decisions.

The Model

Our soil classification model is built using TensorFlow and Keras, with a CNN architecture that includes:

  • Multiple convolutional layers for feature extraction
  • Max pooling layers for spatial dimensionality reduction
  • Dropout for regularization to prevent overfitting
  • Dense layers for final classification

The model is trained on diverse soil images from different regions and conditions to ensure robust performance in real-world scenarios.

How It Works

  1. Image Upload: Users upload an image of soil via the web interface
  2. Preprocessing: The image is resized and normalized
  3. Classification: Our CNN model analyzes the image and identifies the soil type
  4. Recommendations: Based on the identified soil type, suitable crops and farming practices are suggested

The entire process takes just seconds, providing instant insights for better agricultural decisions.

Soil Types We Identify

Black Soil

Rich in clay, calcium, potassium and magnesium. Retains moisture well and is excellent for deep-rooted crops.

Suitable crops:

Cotton
Soybean
Maize
Millets

Cinder Soil

Consists of volcanic materials, typically found in volcanic regions. Light texture with good drainage but may require additional nutrients.

Suitable crops:

Millets
Oilseeds

Laterite Soil

Red in color due to high iron oxide content. Common in tropical areas with high rainfall. Tends to be acidic and leached of nutrients.

Suitable crops:

Tea
Coffee
Cashew

Peat Soil

High in organic matter, retains moisture extremely well. Darker in color and generally acidic. Good for specific crops with proper management.

Suitable crops:

Rice
Potatoes
Sugar Beet

Yellow Soil

Contains iron oxides, often found in regions with moderate rainfall. Relatively less fertile than black soil but supports various crops.

Suitable crops:

Groundnut
Maize
Cotton
